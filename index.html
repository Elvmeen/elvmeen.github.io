Aminu Muhammad Auwal

I am a cybersecurity researcher and academic writer focused on sociological and criminological aspects of cybercrime, digital victimization, and emerging threats within low-resource environments. My work investigates how awareness, mitigation strategies, and policy gaps shape cyber threat exposure—especially in Nigerian and African contexts.

Research

My research focuses on web application security, sociological impacts of cybercrime, threat modeling, and digital criminology. I’m especially interested in how awareness and mitigation strategies vary among developers, administrators, and DevOps professionals in resource-constrained environments. My recent studies combine qualitative research with technical vulnerability analysis—focusing on issues such as directory brute-forcing, exposed Git artifacts, and shell history leakage.

Sociological and Criminological Research of Victimization Issues: Preliminary Stage and New Sphere of Cybercrime Categorization
A. M. Auwal, S. Lazarus
Journal of Digital Technology & Law, Vol 2, No 4 (2024)
DOI / HTML / PDF

This paper explores the evolving categorization of cybercrime and the patterns of victimization emerging in digital spaces. It introduces new theoretical perspectives from criminology into the discussion of cybersecurity.
