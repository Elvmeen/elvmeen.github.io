Aminu Muhammad Auwal

Aminu Muhammad Auwal (also known as Elameen/Elvmeen) is a researcher whose work spans the field of cybersecurity, with a focus on cybercrime, digital victimization, and emerging threats in low-resource environments. With an academic background spanning two degrees — a Bachelor's in Information Technology and a Bachelor's in Physics — his research encompasses web application security, threat modeling, and the application of artificial intelligence in security and ethical contexts.

Research & Publications

Focusing on both foundational and applied cybersecurity challenges, his research bridges theoretical understanding and practical solutions — exploring how digital systems can be strengthened against evolving threats, informing best practices, guiding policy development, and enhancing resilience across diverse environments.

A qualitative exploration of social and emotional consequences faced by victims of financial cybercrimes perpetrated by "Yahoo boys," revealing systemic gaps in support and justice frameworks.
