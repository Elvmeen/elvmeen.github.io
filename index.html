Aminu Muhammad Auwal

Aminu Muhammad Auwal (also known as Elameen/Elvmeen) is a researcher whose work spans the field of cybersecurity, with a focus on cybercrime, digital victimization, and emerging threats in low-resource environments. With an academic background spanning two degrees, a Bachelor’s Degree in Information Technology and another Bachelor’s Degree in Physics, his research encompasses web application security, threat modeling, and the application of artificial intelligence in security and ethical contexts, with particular interest in how AI tools influence vulnerability, detection, and policy responses.

Research & Publications

Focusing on both foundational and applied cybersecurity and cybercrime challenges, his research spans various sections of social and technical aspect of cybercrime and cybersecurity. His work bridges theoretical understanding and practical solutions in cybersecurity, exploring how digital systems can be strengthened against evolving threats. Combining rigorous research with real-world applications, he contributes insights that inform best practices, guide policy development, and enhance the resilience of digital infrastructures across diverse environments.
